Meet Project 13:


Alex Madjar has spent the last four years learning what makes good code. A veteran of Google and Microsoft, he's helped build and test code that reaches millions. It is his involvement in machine learning and interaction research, however, that taught him that good code adapts to its users. Alex has studied at Northwestern University and the Queen Mary University of London.

Josiah Matlack is a junior computer science major in the McCormick School of Engineering and Applied Science. He hails from Hudson, Ohio. He plans to obtain a Master's in Computer Science, and specializes in systems and security. He has interned for PepsiCo Inc., and is currently a Lead Consultant at Northwestern University IT.

Marisa Paulson is a Master of Science in Journalism candidate at the Medill School of Journalism and expects to graduate in August 2012, focusing on interactive media and media management. She earned her B.A. in journalism and mass media communication at Concordia College in Moorhead, Minn., in 2011. She has worked for the American Dental Association, Sanford Health and U.S. Senator Kent Conrad.

Ali Pisano is a Master of Science in Journalism candidate at the Medill School of Journalism and expects to graduate in August 2012, focusing on interactive media and media management. Before coming to Medill, she worked as an editorial assistant at Free Press, a division of Simon & Schuster. She graduated from the University of Virginia in 2009 with a B.A. in Classics and History.

About Project 13

Project 13 started in the spring of 2012 in the course "Collaboration in Journalism and Technology" at Northwestern University. This class brought together two computer science and two journalism students together to create Refine in ten weeks. Project 13 is Alex Madjar, Josiah Matlack, Marisa Paulson, and Ali Pisano.
