Refining the commenting experience:

What if you could sort the comments on the news articles you read online to filter out everything except what truly matters to you?

Introducing Refine, a widget that filters comments based on topics you select.

What is it exactly?

Refine is website tool that filters comments from popular sites into a friendly, readable format. Refine uses machine-learning to categorizes comments visually based on keywords. For websites that use the popular Disqus comment system, like CNN, Refine can highlight relevant or insightful comments. Such comments can often be lost in the noise of thousands upon thousands of user posts; Refine works to condense this content into meaningful keywords.

What's in it for you?

With Refine, users will not have to scroll through many comments to find the few they wanted to read. Keywords will appear below the article text in a word cloud, which users can click on to filter the comment stream by topic.

What's the end result?

By showing news consumers the comments that are most relevant and insightful to them, news organizations can offer a more personalized experience and inspire users to continue their contributions to the online commenting community.

About Project 13

Project 13 started in the spring of 2012 in the course "Collaboration in Journalism and Technology" at Northwestern University. This class brought together two computer science and two journalism students together to create Refine in ten weeks. Project 13 is Alex Madjar, Josiah Matlack, Marisa Paulson, and Ali Pisano.
